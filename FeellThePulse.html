Feel the Pulse

Notes on Building

We opened a WhatsApp group early so users could join directly. What we get is a self-selected mix: the super engaged and the super frustrated. Both tell you things analytics can't.

So far, less than 1% of users have joined. That's enough. You don't need statistical significance when you're listening for signal, not measuring effect sizes.

The group surfaces bugs that would otherwise stay invisible. Here's one: the entire dev team reinstalls the app constantly—testing, debugging, resetting state. We never experience what a long-term user experiences. A user in the group reported that after solving more than 1,000 puzzles, the app started lagging. FPS dropped noticeably.

The cause was a memory leak that only manifested at scale. We would have found it eventually, maybe through a one-star review, maybe through churn we couldn't explain. Instead, we found it through someone who cared enough to tell us directly.

This is what "feeling the pulse" means. Not dashboards. Not surveys. A channel where your most invested users can reach you, and where their frustration becomes your information advantage.
